List of R, python and statistics resources

R

Beginners, entry-level

  • Data Camp: A website with clear courses to build your R knowledge from the ground up with interactive exercice

  • An introduction to R: The official R introduction written by those who helped creating and maintaining R

Intermediate, consolidating the foundations

  • The Art of R Programming: A nice (free) book providing ample knowledge to understand how to programm in R

  • R for data science: A free e-book from Hadley Wickham the dplyr/ggplot2 gouru with a clear layout to master R workflow from data import to building models and producing nice figures

Advanced, when you start writing your own functions

  • Writing R extensions: the official manual on creating packages, linking C++ to R ...

  • R packages: Hadley Wickham's book on writing R packages

Statistics

Beginners

Intermediate

  • Ecological models and data in R: A great book by Ben Bolker one of the lme4 maintainer, this book allows you to peak under the hood of typical models and will give you basic understanding of Maximum Likelihood Estimation.

  • Data Analysis Using Regression and Multilevel/Hierarchical Models: Those who want to know more about mixed-effect models should check out this book.

  • Statistical rethinking: A gentle and applied introduction to the Bayesian framework, you will be surprised how fast you can go from linear models to gaussian mixtures ...

Hard, only for hard-core stats geeks

  • Bayesian data analysis: the bible for any bayesians aficionados.
